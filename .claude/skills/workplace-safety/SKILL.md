## Critical Requirements

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
**Required PPE:**
- Hard hats at all times on construction sites
- Safety glasses or goggles for eye protection
- Hearing protection >85 dBA
- Safety gloves appropriate for task
- Steel-toed boots
- High-visibility clothing in traffic areas
- Respiratory protection when needed

**Employer Duties:**
- Provide PPE at no cost
- Ensure proper fit and training
- Maintain and replace damaged PPE

### Fire Safety (Sections 52-58)
**Requirements:**
- Fire extinguishers accessible and maintained
- Clear access to fire exits
- Exit signs illuminated
- No storage blocking exits or fire equipment
- Flammable materials stored properly
- No smoking policies enforced
- Regular fire drills

### Hot Work (Sections 122-124)
**Permit Required:**
- Hot work permits for welding, cutting, grinding
- Fire watch assigned
- Combustibles removed within 11m (35 feet)
- Fire extinguisher present
- Fire watch continues 30 minutes after work stops
- Ventilation for fume control

### Demolition (Sections 212-221)
**Requirements:**
- Engineered demolition procedure required
- Notice to Ministry for buildings ≥4m high or ≥30m² floor area
- Top-down sequence (work from top to bottom)
- All utilities isolated before work
- Asbestos/hazmat assessment completed first
- No workers below demolition area
- Dust control measures
- Structural stability maintained

### Hard Hats
**Selection:**
- CSA Z94.1 certified
- Class E (electrical protection) recommended
- Replace after impact or every 5 years
- Inspect for cracks, dents, or UV damage
- No modifications (stickers may be allowed if approved)
- Proper adjustment and fit

### Respiratory Protection
**Requirements:**
- Medical evaluation before use
- **Fit testing required** for tight-fitting respirators
- Annual fit testing and retest if facial changes
- Training on proper use and limitations
- Types:
  - N95/P100 for particulates (dust, silica)
  - Chemical cartridges for vapors/gases
  - SCBA for oxygen-deficient atmospheres
- Clean and store properly

### Incident Reporting
**OHSA Requirements:**
- Critical injury: Notify Ministry immediately
- Fatality: Notify Ministry immediately
- Occupational illness: Within 4 days
- Preserve scene until Ministry inspector releases
- Written report within 48 hours
- Incident investigation required

**Reportable Incidents Include:**
- Fatalities
- Critical injuries (fractures, amputations, burns, unconsciousness)
- Occupational illnesses

### Legal Duties (OHSA)

**Employer Duties:**
- Provide safe workplace and equipment
- Provide information, instruction, supervision
- Ensure compliance with OHSA and regulations
- Appoint competent supervisors
- Maintain safety programs

**Supervisor Duties:**
- Ensure workers use PPE
- Advise workers of hazards
- Take every reasonable precaution
- Ensure compliance with OHSA

**Worker Duties:**
- Follow OHSA and regulations
- Use PPE as required
- Report hazards and defective equipment
- Not endanger others

**Worker Rights:**
- Right to know about hazards
- Right to participate in safety
- Right to refuse unsafe work

## How to Respond

When answering questions:

1. **Identify the specific safety topic**
   - PPE, fire, hot work, demolition, or general safety
   - Assess regulatory requirements

2. **Cite specific regulations**
   - O. Reg 213/91 section numbers
   - OHSA sections for legal duties
   - CSA standards for equipment

3. **Provide practical guidance**
   - Step-by-step procedures
   - Inspection requirements
   - Training needs
   - Documentation requirements

4. **Include hierarchy of controls where applicable**
   - Engineering controls first
   - Administrative controls
   - PPE as last line of defense

5. **Emphasize critical safety points**
   - Legal duty implications
   - Incident reporting requirements
   - Fit testing for respirators mandatory
   - Hard hats required at all times
   - Fire watch procedures
